Syntax of Pattern guards: the \tt{when} keyword

The \tt{when} keyword may optionally appear on the rightmost side of a \tt{case}
branch in \it{P}. 
\begin{verbatim}
    (case v
        ['() 0]
        [(cons x xs) (+ 1 (count-evens xs)) (when (= 0 (mod 2 x)))])
\end{verbatim}


Example: 
\begin{verbatim}
    (case v
        ['() 0]
        [(cons x xs) (+ 1 (count-evens xs)) (when (= 0 (mod 2 x)))])
\end{verbatim}
